Hong Shao Rou

Hong Shao Rou or red cooked pork is one of my favorite Chinese dishes, it's what I ask for everytime I visit mom back home. It's sweet, its savoury, it soothes the soul. Today I'll be teaching you how to make this amazing dish.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces lean, skin-on pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon rock sugar
  • 3 tablespoon cooking wine
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1/2 tablesoon dark soy sauce
  • 2 cups water

Directions

  1. Cut pork belly into 3/4-inch thick pieces
  2. Bring water to a boil. Blanch the pork belly for a couple minutes, scope out the impurities. Take pork out, rinse, and set aside.
  3. Low heat, add oil and sugar to wok. Melt the sugar slightly before adding the pork. Raise the heat to medium and cook until the pork is lightly browned.
  4. Turn the heat back down to low, add cooking wine, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and water.
  5. Cover and simmer over medium heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour until pork is fork tender.
  6. Stir every 5-10 minutes to prevent burning, add water if it gets too dry.
  7. Once fork tender, uncover the work, turn up the heat, and stir continuously until the sauce has reduced to a glistening coating.
