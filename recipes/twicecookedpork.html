Hui Guo Rou

Twice Cooked Pork, or hui guo rou (回鍋肉), is a Sichuan dish of spicy seared pork belly that’s way too famous to ignore and too delicious not to share. If you’ve never tried it, you NEED to make this recipe. It is simply and unequivocally delicious!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork belly
  • 2 slices ginger
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons spicy broad bean paste
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 long hot green peppers
  • 1 medium leek
  • 1 tablespoon shaoxing wine
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar

Directions

  1. Bring 2 quarts of water to boil. Add meat and ginger, and bring pot to boil again. Lower heat and simmer for 30 minutes till tender. Remove pork and rinse under cold water
  2. Thinly slice pork belly to about 1/8 inch thick.
  3. Heat wok over high until smoke. Add 1 tablespoon of oil and sear the pork till light caramelization. Turn the heat to medium-low and scoop out the pork.
  4. Add another tablespoon of oil and spicy bean paste, fry for 30 seconds. Avoid burning.
  5. Add garlic and fry for 30 seconds. Turn the heat to high and add pork, peppers and leeks. Stir-fry for a minute. Add shaoxing wine, soy sauce and sugar. Stir everything together. Once veggies are cooked its ready to serve.
